Setter Penetration Rules

📍 What is Setter Penetration?

Setter penetration is a key technique in volleyball. When the setter rotates to the back row, they must quickly move to the front row (between positions 2-3) after the serve to maintain the ideal setting position.

🎯 The 6 Court Positions
④ Left Front
③ Middle Front
② Right Front
══════ NET ══════
⑤ Left Back
⑥ Middle Back
① Right Back

Front row: positions 2, 3, 4 (attack near the net)
Back row: positions 1, 5, 6 (mainly defense)

🔄 Front Row vs Back Row

✅ Setter in Front Row

Ideal position — can set directly without penetrating

⚠️ Setter in Back Row

Too far from the net — must penetrate to front row after the serve

💡 Why Penetrate?
1 Maintain the best setting position to feed OH and MB
2 React quickly to improve attack efficiency
3 Keep the offensive system consistent every rotation
⏰ When to Penetrate?

⚡ Penetrate immediately after the serve!

Note: Before the serve, back-row players must stay behind their corresponding front-row players. Movement is free after the serve.
